--- PROMPT ---
Walk through a concrete example: a household earning $90,000/year considering a $450,000 home. What changes between a 3% and a 6.37% mortgage?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
To understand how rising mortgage interest rates affect the affordability of a $450,000 home with an annual income of $90,000, we can perform a 
detailed analysis:

1. **Monthly Mortgage Payment Calculation:**
   - **3% Annual Interest Rate:**
     Using the formula:
     \[
     M = P \frac{r(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n - 1}
     \]
     where \( P = \$450,000 \), \( r = 0.03 / 12 = 0.0025 \), and \( n = 360 \):
     \[
     M = 450,000 \frac{0.0025(1+0.0025)^{360}}{(1+0.0025)^{360} - 1} \approx \$2,201.95
     \]
   
   - **6.37% Annual Interest Rate:**
     Similarly, calculating for \( r = 0.0637 / 12 \approx 0.005308 \):
     \[
     M = 450,000 \frac{0.005308(1+0.005308)^{360}}{(1+0.005308)^{360} - 1} \approx \$3,488
     \]

2. **Monthly Payment Difference:**
   The difference between the two monthly payments is approximately:
   \[
   \$3,488 - \$2,201.95 = \$1,286.05
   \]
   This means an extra $1,286 per month in mortgage payments with a 6.37% rate compared to a 3% rate.

3. **Impact on Affordability:**
   - At the 3% rate:
     - Total monthly payment: \$2,201.95
     - Remaining income after mortgage: \$7,500 / 12 - \$2,201.95 ≈ \$4,012 per month

   - At the 6.37% rate:
     - Total monthly payment: \$3,488
     - Remaining income after mortgage: \$7,500 / 12 - \$3,488 ≈ \$4,203 per month

4. **Additional Costs:**
   Considering additional expenses such as property taxes (assumed at about 1-3% of the home price annually), insurance, and maintenance:
   - Property taxes: \$4,500 to \$13,500 annually
   - Insurance: Assumed around \$150 annually

   Total additional monthly costs could be approximately \$296.

5. **Total Monthly Cost at Different Rates:**
   - At the 3% rate:
     \[
     \$2,201.95 + \$296 = \$2,497.95
     \]
   
   - At the 6.37% rate:
     \[
     \$3,488 + \$296 = \$3,784
     \]

6. **Conclusion:**
   The higher interest rate significantly increases the monthly mortgage payment and total cost of owning the home. While the difference in payments 
is substantial, additional expenses further reduce the remaining income for other needs.

In summary, an increase from a 3% to a 6.37% mortgage interest rate leads to a considerable rise in monthly mortgage costs, reducing overall 
affordability unless financial strategies like refinancing or adjusting income are considered.
