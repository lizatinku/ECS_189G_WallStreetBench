--- PROMPT ---
How can a stock index hit new highs while real consumer purchasing power has fallen? Reconcile these two facts.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The situation where a stock market index hits new highs while real consumer purchasing power falls can be reconciled by considering the interplay between various economic factors:

1. **Stock Market Performance**: An increase in the stock market indicates strong corporate earnings and positive investor sentiment, often linked to economic growth.

2. **Inflation Dynamics**: A rise in inflation, driven by factors such as supply chain issues, wage increases, or government policies, can erode purchasing power despite nominal income gains.

3. **Economic Growth and Employment**: Higher corporate profits can lead to job creation and increased consumer income, but if inflation rises faster than income growth, real purchasing power may still decline.

4. **Consumer Spending**: While higher earnings might encourage spending, the actual impact on purchasing power depends on how inflation affects price levels across goods and services.

- Although companies' profitability and corporate profits suggest economic expansion, rapid inflation can offset this by increasing prices faster than income growth.
