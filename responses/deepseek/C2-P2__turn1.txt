--- PROMPT ---
Which of these three pass-throughs typically lags the most, and why?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The transmission of Federal Reserve policy rate cuts impacts different sectors with varying lags:

1. **Credit Card APRs**: These are directly influenced by bank lending practices, allowing for immediate adjustments as banks pass savings on to consumers.

2. **Auto Loan Rates**: Dependent on the Fed's rates through banks but experience a slight lag due to the intermediary role of car dealers in securing funds from banks.

3. **Long-Duration Tech Stocks**: These are indirectly affected by policy rate cuts, as their capital costs decrease when borrowing becomes cheaper, but this impact may not fully translate into stock price changes immediately due to factors like market sentiment and industry-specific news.

In conclusion, long-duration tech stocks typically exhibit the longest lag after Fed rate cuts because their connection is indirect and influenced by multiple external factors.
