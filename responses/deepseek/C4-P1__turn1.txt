The strengthening of the U.S. dollar against other currencies has significant impacts on international students, particularly in terms of purchasing power and savings 
when converting their USD back to their local currency. Here's a structured summary:

1. **Impact on International Students:**
   - **Positive Impact:** The exchange rate allows for more flexibility in managing foreign currency requirements.
   - **Negative Impact:** A stronger dollar reduces the purchasing power of the saved USD when converted back to the home currency, potentially increasing costs upon 
return.

2. **Concrete Example:**
   - Suppose an international student has $10,000 USD saved up and returns home after studies in a country where the U.S. dollar strengthens.
   - **Initial Exchange Rate:** 1 USD = 7.46 CNY (in January 2023).
     - $10,000 USD converts to 74,600 CNY.
   - **After Appreciation:** 1 USD = 7.85 CNY (in March 2023).
     - $10,000 USD now converts to 78,500 CNY.

3. **Analysis:**
   - While the total amount in CNY increases due to the appreciation of the dollar, this does not necessarily translate into increased purchasing power for the student.
   - The initial lower value (74,600 CNY) represents a more valuable savings when converted back home before the currency strengthening.

In conclusion, while an increase in USD's value may seem beneficial at first glance, it can lead to higher costs upon return due to reduced purchasing power of 
previously saved funds.
