--- PROMPT ---
The Federal Reserve has cut its policy rate by 75 basis points over the past nine months. How might this transmit to (a) credit card APRs, (b) auto loan rates, and (c) the price of long-duration tech stocks? Explain which links are direct and which are indirect.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The Federal Reserve's 75 basis point cut in its policy rate over nine months has significant implications across various sectors, each with distinct transmission mechanisms:

1. **Credit Card APRs**: 
   - **Direct Transmission**: The Fed's policy rate directly influences credit card Annual Percentage Rates (APRs). Banks, which offer these rates, have a direct cost tied to the federal funds rate. A 75 basis point cut reduces their borrowing costs, allowing them to pass savings onto consumers by lowering APRs.
   - **Lag Effect**: However, this change isn't immediate; banks may adjust rates with some delay as they assess and implement new conditions.

2. **Auto Loan Rates**:
