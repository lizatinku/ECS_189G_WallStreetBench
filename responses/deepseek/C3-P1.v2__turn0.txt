Why do companies sometimes see their stock prices go up on layoff announcements? Use the recent Coinbase / Oracle / Atlassian examples.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The phenomenon where companies' stock prices increase following layoff announcements can be attributed to several key factors:

1. **Operational Efficiency and Cost Savings**: Companies may achieve significant cost savings by eliminating unnecessary roles, allowing them to focus on core operations and potentially improve margins or cash flow.

2. **Strategic Restructuring**: Layoffs often signal strategic changes aimed at aligning the company's structure with future growth plans, positioning it for long-term success.

3. **Investor Perception of Management Confidence**: Positive announcements from management about restructuring can enhance investor confidence, viewing the moves as indicators of a clear strategy and future performance expectations.

4. **Risk Management During Uncertain Times**: In challenging economic conditions, layoffs may be seen as risk mitigation strategies to control expenses and maintain profitability.

5. **Potential for Future Growth**: Companies might announce layoffs to focus on high-growth areas or innovation, positioning themselves for increased future earnings despite current job cuts.
