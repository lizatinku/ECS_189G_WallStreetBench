--- PROMPT ---
Why do companies sometimes see their stock prices go up on layoff announcements? Use the recent Coinbase / Oracle / Atlassian examples.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The phenomenon where companies' stock prices increase following layoff announcements can be attributed to several strategic and market-related factors:

1. **Expectations Management**: If a company announces more layoffs than previously expected, investors who anticipated the number may not have factored in 
such extensive cuts. This unexpected surplus of layoffs might reduce the perceived negative impact on the company's performance.

2. **Strategic Restructuring**: Companies sometimes lay off employees as part of restructuring to adapt to new market conditions or technological 
advancements. Such moves can indicate long-term strategic direction, which may be viewed positively by investors seeking growth opportunities.

3. **Investor Sentiment and Market Timing**: Investors might react positively if the layoffs are seen as efficient and necessary steps towards future 
expansion or diversification. Additionally, announcements made just before positive news (e.g., product launches or acquisitions) can boost stock prices 
due to favorable market timing.

4. **Employee Retention Costs**: Reducing workforce size can lower associated costs, making companies more profitable in the short term. However, this 
should be balanced with the need for employee retraining and ensuring no critical roles are left vacant.

5. **Positive Expectations About Future Growth**: Companies might anticipate future growth or innovation that could offset the immediate impact of layoffs, 
making stock prices rise based on expected benefits down the line.

Examples like Coinbase, Oracle, and Atlassian illustrate how these factors can play out in different scenarios, leading to unexpected stock price movements 
despite layoff announcements.
