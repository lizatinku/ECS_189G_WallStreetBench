--- PROMPT ---
A graduate student is deciding between (i) renting at $1,800/month in a city with 4% annual rent growth or (ii) buying a $320,000 condo at a 6.5% mortgage with 10% down. Walk through the key factors that would tip the decision one way or the other, and where uncertainty matters most.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The decision between renting and buying a graduate student graduate involves several key factors:

1. **Monthly Costs**: Renting at $1,800 per month is significantly cheaper than buying, which requires monthly mortgage payments of around $1,820 plus additional 
expenses totaling approximately $2,720.

2. **Opportunity Cost**: By purchasing, the graduate forgoes potential investment returns from a $320k down payment, which could grow at 7% annually compared to the 
current mortgage rate and possible rental benefits.

3. **Property Appreciation**: The house may appreciate faster than rent increases or mortgage rates if local economic conditions support it. However, this depends on 
market performance beyond given assumptions.

4. **Uncertainty**: Future real estate returns, interest rates, and investment opportunities are uncertain factors that can influence the decision.

**Conclusion**: Renting is more cost-effective in the short term due to lower monthly expenses and no upfront capital requirement. Buying may be preferable if the 
graduate expects significant property appreciation or if they plan to stay long-term and benefit from potential equity growth, despite higher initial costs. The decision 
should weigh these factors against personal financial goals, risk tolerance, and market outlook.

\boxed{\text{Consider short-term cash flow for renting; evaluate long-term benefits and risks for buying.}}
