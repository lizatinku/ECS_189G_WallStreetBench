--- PROMPT ---
Suppose I also told you the S&P 500 rose 7% in the same month. Does that change your interpretation, and why?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
Yes. It changes the interpretation by adding an important benchmark.

Tesla rose about **21.6%**, while the S&P 500 rose **7%** over the same month. That means Tesla **outperformed the broader market by roughly 14.6 percentage points**.

This suggests two things:

1. **Part of Tesla’s move may reflect broad market strength.**
   A rising S&P 500 indicates that investor sentiment or macro conditions were generally favorable, so Tesla’s gain may not be entirely company-specific.

2. **Tesla likely had additional stock-specific or sector-specific momentum.**
   Since Tesla rose much more than the market, the move may also reflect factors unique to Tesla, high-growth stocks, EV/AI sentiment, short covering, or stronger risk appetite toward more volatile names.

The confidence improves slightly in saying **broad market conditions contributed**, because the benchmark also rose. But confidence remains limited in identifying the exact cause of Tesla’s extra outperformance without more evidence, such as peer stock moves, volume, news, earnings, analyst revisions, or factor data.

So the revised interpretation is: **Tesla participated in a strong market rally but also showed substantial relative outperformance, suggesting broader market strength was probably not the whole explanation.**
