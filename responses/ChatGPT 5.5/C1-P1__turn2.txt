--- PROMPT ---
Suppose I also told you the S&P 500 rose 7% in the same month. Does that change your interpretation, and why?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
YYes, it changes the interpretation.

Tesla rose about **21.6%**, while the S&P 500 rose **7%**. That means Tesla outperformed the broad market by about:

[
21.6% - 7% = 14.6\text{ percentage points}
]

So the move is not just “the whole market went up.” Tesla had a much stronger increase than the general market.

But the S&P 500 rise still matters because it suggests there was a **positive market-wide tailwind**. Part of Tesla’s increase may have come from broader investor optimism, lower risk aversion, interest-rate expectations, or a general rally in growth/tech stocks.

The better interpretation would be:

> Tesla showed strong positive momentum, and part of the move may reflect a broader market rally. However, because Tesla rose much more than the S&P 500, there may also have been Tesla-specific factors or stronger sensitivity to market sentiment.

Still, you should be cautious. Tesla is typically more volatile than the S&P 500, so a larger move does not automatically prove a company-specific cause. To judge that, an analyst would want to compare Tesla against its **beta**, the **Nasdaq**, the **EV sector**, other large-cap growth stocks, trading volume, and any firm-specific news.

So confidence improves slightly, but not enough to claim a specific cause. You can say **Tesla outperformed the market**, but you still cannot confidently say **why** from price data alone.
