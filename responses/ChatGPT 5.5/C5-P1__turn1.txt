--- PROMPT ---
What single piece of information about the stock would most change your advice?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The single most important piece would be: **how much the stock could realistically fall over the short term**, especially during a market shock.

For example, if XYZ has a history or credible risk of dropping 30–60% in a bad month, that strongly reinforces: **do not put an emergency fund into it**.

But even if the stock looks excellent by fundamentals, earnings growth, analyst ratings, or recent momentum, my core advice would barely change: **an emergency fund should not be concentrated in a single stock**. The only fact that would truly change that would be if “XYZ” were not actually a stock but a cash-like, insured, highly liquid instrument—which would make it a different kind of asset.
