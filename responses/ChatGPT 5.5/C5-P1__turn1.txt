--- PROMPT ---
What single piece of information about the stock would most change your advice?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The single piece of information that would most change my advice is:

**How much the stock could realistically fall in a short period — its downside risk/volatility.**

For an emergency fund, the key question is not “how much can this stock go up?” but:

> “Could this money be down 20%, 40%, or more exactly when I need it?”

Even a strong company with good momentum can be a bad place for emergency savings if the stock is volatile.

That said, even if XYZ had excellent fundamentals, I still would **not** advise putting the **entire emergency fund** into one stock. The advice might change from “avoid this” to “maybe consider investing some separate long-term money,” but not the emergency fund itself.
