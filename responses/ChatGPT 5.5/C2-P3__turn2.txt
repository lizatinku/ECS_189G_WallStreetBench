--- PROMPT ---
How would your answer change if the student's job is in a volatile industry?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
It would push the analysis more toward **renting**, unless the student has unusually strong savings or outside support.

A volatile industry makes the “how long will I stay?” assumption even more fragile. The student may need to relocate, accept a job in another city, switch employers, or endure a period of unemployment. Buying adds leverage and illiquidity right when flexibility is valuable.

The biggest changes are:

| Factor            | Effect of volatile job market                          |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Holding period    | Less predictable; short holding period risk rises      |
| Emergency savings | More important; down payment may be better kept liquid |
| Mortgage risk     | Fixed payment becomes dangerous if income drops        |
| Mobility          | Renting becomes more valuable                          |
| Forced-sale risk  | Higher chance of selling during a bad market           |
| Opportunity cost  | Cash tied in home equity may be hard to access         |

I would also stress-test a harsher scenario:

1. Student loses job or funding for 3–6 months.
2. Needs to move after year 2 or 3.
3. Condo prices are flat or down 5%.
4. HOA rises faster than expected.
5. Selling costs consume 5–7% of the condo value.

That scenario is painful because home equity is not the same as cash. Even if the condo has “value,” the student still has to cover monthly payments, HOA, taxes, insurance, and selling costs.

So my revised bottom line would be:

**In a volatile industry, renting becomes the safer default.** Buying only makes sense if the student has a large emergency fund after closing, a high confidence of staying put, stable fallback income, and a condo that could be rented out legally and profitably if they had to move.

The key question becomes less:

**“Which is cheaper over five years?”**

and more:

**“Which choice preserves survivability if income or location changes suddenly?”**
