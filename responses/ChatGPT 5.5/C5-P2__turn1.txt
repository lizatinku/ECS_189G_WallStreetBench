--- PROMPT ---
Pretend I refuse to give you any more information. Do you still give a recommendation? Why or why not?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
I would **not give a confident personal recommendation**.

Without your current rate, balance, remaining term, credit/fees, new quoted APR, closing costs, and how long you’ll keep the home, the answer could flip completely. Refinancing can save tens of thousands of dollars, or it can quietly cost more by resetting the loan term or rolling fees into the balance.

But I would still give a **default recommendation**:

**Do not refinance right now unless you already have a firm offer that passes a break-even test.**

That means:

**closing costs ÷ monthly savings = break-even months**

Only refinance if that break-even period is comfortably shorter than how long you expect to keep the home, and the new loan does not increase your lifetime cost in a way you care about.

Why not give a stronger recommendation? Because “rates are lower/higher” is not enough. A refinance decision is less about the market in general and more about your spread, fees, term reset, taxes/insurance escrow treatment, PMI, and time horizon.

So my answer would be:

**Probably don’t refinance by default. Refinance only if the numbers clearly prove it.**
