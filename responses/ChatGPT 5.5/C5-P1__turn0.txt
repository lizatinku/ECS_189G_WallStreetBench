=== Prompt ID: C5-P1  |  Category: C5  |  Turn: 0 ===

--- PROMPT ---
A friend says: 'Stock XYZ has gone up every month for the last six months — I should put my entire emergency fund into it.' Evaluate this reasoning.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
Your friend’s reasoning is flawed.

A stock going up every month for six months is **not strong evidence that it will keep going up**. This is a form of **trend-chasing** or **recency bias**: giving too much weight to recent performance and assuming the pattern will continue. It can also reflect the **hot-hand fallacy**, where a streak is mistaken for a reliable signal.

The bigger issue is the emergency fund. An emergency fund is meant to be **safe, liquid, and available when needed**. Individual stocks are volatile; even a good company can drop sharply because of earnings, market panic, interest rates, lawsuits, sector rotation, or bad news. Putting the entire emergency fund into one stock creates **concentration risk** and could force them to sell at a loss during an emergency.

A better approach would be:

Emergency fund: keep in cash, high-yield savings, money market fund, or similarly low-risk liquid account.

Investing money: only use money they can afford to leave invested for years, ideally diversified rather than concentrated in one stock.

So the conclusion “it has risen for six months, therefore I should put my whole emergency fund into it” does **not** follow. The past trend might justify researching the stock, but it does not justify risking essential savings.
