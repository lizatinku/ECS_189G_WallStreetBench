--- PROMPT ---
Coinbase, Oracle Corporation, and Atlassian announced layoffs despite continued investment in AI and technology growth. How might layoffs affect investor perception and stock prices? Why can stock prices sometimes increase even after companies reduce their workforce?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
Layoffs can affect investor perception in mixed ways. For companies like Coinbase, Oracle, and Atlassian, the market usually looks less at the headline “layoffs” and more at what the cuts imply about future earnings, strategy, and demand.

Layoffs may hurt investor perception when they suggest:

* weaker demand or slowing revenue growth
* management overhired or misallocated capital
* pressure on margins or cash flow
* reduced innovation capacity, especially if technical talent is cut
* lower employee morale and higher execution risk

But layoffs can also improve investor sentiment when they are seen as:

* cost discipline
* a shift toward higher-margin priorities, such as AI, cloud, automation, or enterprise software
* a way to protect profitability during slower growth
* evidence that management is willing to make difficult efficiency decisions
* a path to better free cash flow and earnings per share

That is why stock prices can sometimes rise after layoffs. Investors often value future profits more than current headcount. If a company announces workforce reductions while saying it will keep investing in AI or strategic technology areas, the market may interpret that as “cutting lower-priority costs while funding growth areas.”

A simplified example:

A company reduces headcount by 8%, saving hundreds of millions in annual expenses. Even if revenue growth is slower, analysts may raise profit forecasts because operating costs are falling. Higher expected margins can increase valuation, so the stock may rise.

However, this reaction is not guaranteed. If layoffs look defensive, desperate, or tied to collapsing demand, the stock can fall. The key question investors ask is:

“Are these layoffs a sign of strategic efficiency, or a sign that the business is weakening?”

In short, layoffs can boost stock prices when investors believe they will improve profitability without damaging long-term growth. They can hurt stock prices when investors believe they reflect deeper business problems.
