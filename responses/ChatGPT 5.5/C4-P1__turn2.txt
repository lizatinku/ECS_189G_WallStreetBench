--- PROMPT ---
Which group is hurt most quickly, and which experiences the impact with the longest lag?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
**Hurt most quickly: international students.**
They feel the impact almost immediately when they exchange home currency into U.S. dollars to pay tuition, rent, food, insurance, or fees. A weaker home currency means the same dollar bill costs more right away. The effect can show up within days or weeks, especially around tuition-payment deadlines.

**Longest lag: multinational companies.**
Many large firms hedge currency exposure, set contracts in advance, and adjust prices only gradually. The stronger dollar may not fully appear in earnings until future quarters, when foreign revenue is translated back into dollars or when new contracts, pricing decisions, and supply-chain costs reset.

Import/export businesses are in the middle. Importers and exporters may feel the exchange-rate effect quickly on new orders, but existing contracts, inventory, hedging, and shipping timelines can delay the full impact.
