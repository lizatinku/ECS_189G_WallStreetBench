### What would make Intel stock move much more strongly?

Intel would likely rally more if the announcement includes **real customer commitments**, especially from hyperscalers like Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta, or Oracle. A launch with only specs is less powerful; a launch with purchase orders, deployment dates, or volume targets is much more meaningful.

The strongest version would be: **Intel announces a competitive AI accelerator, priced below NVIDIA, with strong memory bandwidth, clear software support, and named cloud customers already deploying it.** In that case:

* **INTC:** strong positive reaction
* **AMD:** more negative, because AMD’s “AI alternative” positioning weakens
* **NVDA:** modest negative, but probably not a collapse unless Intel proves software compatibility and scale

### What would make AMD less vulnerable?

AMD would hold up better if Intel’s product is aimed at a niche, arrives late, or lacks software maturity. AMD already has momentum in EPYC CPUs and Instinct GPUs, so investors may still view AMD as the more realistic second supplier if Intel’s announcement looks early-stage or mostly promotional. ([Advanced Micro Devices, Inc.][2])

### What would make NVIDIA actually fall hard?

NVIDIA would likely fall more only if Intel shows **three things at once**:

1. Performance close to NVIDIA on real AI workloads
2. Meaningfully lower total cost of ownership
3. Major customers willing to shift future orders away from NVIDIA

Without those, NVIDIA may dip briefly but remain supported by its massive Data Center revenue, ecosystem advantage, and current AI infrastructure demand. ([NVIDIA Newsroom][3])

### My final forecast

A simple “major launch” headline: **INTC up, AMD down a little, NVDA roughly flat to slightly down.**

A launch with hyperscaler orders and strong benchmarks: **INTC up a lot, AMD down more, NVDA down modestly.**

A launch with vague timing, no customers, and no software story: **INTC pops then fades; AMD and NVDA barely affected.**
