=== Prompt ID: C1-P1  |  Category: C1  |  Turn: 1 ===

--- PROMPT ---
If you only had the closing prices and no news context, how confident should an analyst be in attributing this move to a specific cause?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
An analyst should have **low confidence** in attributing the move to any specific cause using only closing prices.

Closing prices can show **what happened**—Tesla rose from $352 to $428, about **21.6%**—but they do not show **why** it happened. Without news, volume, intraday trading behavior, sector performance, market index moves, options activity, earnings dates, analyst actions, macro data, or peer comparisons, any causal explanation would be speculative.

A reasonable analyst could say:

> “The stock exhibited strong upward momentum over the period, but the available data are insufficient to identify the driver.”

They might form hypotheses, such as market-wide risk appetite, company-specific expectations, short covering, or sector rotation, but those should be framed as **possible explanations**, not conclusions.

So confidence in the **trend** is high; confidence in a **specific causal attribution** is low.
