--- PROMPT ---
Rewrite the argument so that it correctly uses P/E to make a much weaker, defensible claim.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
A weaker, defensible version would be:

> Tesla’s P/E ratio of 95 is much higher than Ford’s P/E ratio of 7, which suggests that investors are pricing Tesla with much stronger expectations for future growth, profitability, or strategic upside than Ford. This valuation gap does not by itself prove that Tesla is overvalued or that Ford is undervalued, since the two companies differ in growth prospects, margins, business mix, and risk profile. However, the high relative multiple does mean Tesla’s stock may be more vulnerable if its future earnings growth disappoints or if investors reduce the premium they are willing to pay for its expected growth.

Even shorter:

> Tesla’s P/E ratio is far higher than Ford’s, so Tesla appears to be priced with much more optimistic future expectations. This does not prove Tesla is overvalued, but it suggests the stock may carry greater valuation risk if those expectations are not met.
