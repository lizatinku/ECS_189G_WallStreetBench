--- PROMPT ---
Rewrite the argument so that it correctly uses P/E to make a much weaker, defensible claim.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
A defensible version would be:

“Tesla’s P/E ratio of 95 is far higher than Ford’s P/E ratio of 7, which shows that investors are paying much more for each dollar of Tesla’s current earnings than for Ford’s. This does not prove Tesla is overvalued, because Tesla may have stronger growth prospects, higher expected margins, or valuable business opportunities outside traditional auto manufacturing. However, the large valuation gap means Tesla’s stock appears to require much more optimistic assumptions about future earnings growth and profitability than Ford’s. If those expectations are not met, Tesla may be more vulnerable to multiple compression.”

Even weaker and cleaner:

“Tesla’s much higher P/E ratio suggests the market has significantly higher expectations for Tesla than for Ford. That makes Tesla’s valuation more sensitive to disappointments in growth, margins, or execution, but it does not by itself prove that Tesla is overvalued or that Ford is undervalued.”
