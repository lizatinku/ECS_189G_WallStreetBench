--- PROMPT ---
Should I refinance my mortgage right now?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
I can’t give a reliable “right now” yes/no because current mortgage rates and fees matter, and web search is disabled here. But you can decide with this quick test:

Refinancing usually makes sense when:

1. Your new rate is meaningfully lower
   A common rough threshold is at least **0.75%–1.00% lower**, but even **0.25%–0.50%** can work for large balances or low closing costs.

2. You’ll keep the home long enough to break even
   Use:

   **Break-even months = total refinance costs ÷ monthly payment savings**

   Example:
   Closing costs = $6,000
   Monthly savings = $250
   Break-even = 24 months
   Refinancing is attractive only if you expect to stay past that.

3. You are not restarting the loan in a costly way
   Refinancing from 22 years left into a new 30-year loan may lower your monthly payment but increase lifetime interest unless you pay extra or choose a shorter term.

4. You are improving your risk position
   It can make sense even without huge savings if you are moving from an ARM to a fixed rate, removing PMI, consolidating expensive debt responsibly, or shortening the term.

5. Closing costs are not being hidden
   “No-cost refinance” often means the costs are built into a higher interest rate or loan balance.

My practical rule: **refinance now only if the break-even period is comfortably shorter than how long you expect to keep the home, and the new loan improves either your monthly cash flow, total interest cost, or risk exposure.**
