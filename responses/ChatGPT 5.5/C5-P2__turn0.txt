--- PROMPT ---
Should I refinance my mortgage right now?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
Probably **not**, unless your current mortgage rate is meaningfully above today’s refi rates.

As of **May 28, 2026**, national 30-year refinance averages are around **6.75%–6.76%**, with APR around **6.84%**; 15-year refi rates are around **6.08%–6.09%**. Freddie Mac’s latest weekly 30-year fixed mortgage average was **6.51%** as of May 21, 2026. ([Bankrate][1])

My rule of thumb:

* **Current rate below ~6.5%:** usually **do not refinance** right now.
* **Current rate around 6.75%–7.0%:** probably **not worth it**, unless closing costs are very low or you are switching to a shorter term.
* **Current rate above ~7.25%–7.5%:** **worth checking quotes**, especially if you’ll stay in the home long enough to break even.
* **Current rate above 8%:** refinancing is more likely to make sense, but still depends on costs and loan term.

The key calculation is:

**break-even months = closing costs ÷ monthly savings**

For example, if refinancing costs **$6,000** and saves **$200/month**, you break even after **30 months**. This break-even logic is the standard way to judge whether the refinance costs are offset by payment savings. ([Chase][2])

Also be careful: refinancing into a new **30-year loan** may lower your monthly payment but can increase total lifetime interest by resetting the clock. It is better to compare the new loan against your **remaining term**, not just monthly payment.

So my practical answer is: **don’t rush to refinance right now unless your current rate is above about 7.25%, you can get low closing costs, and you plan to keep the home past the break-even point.** Send me your current rate, balance, remaining years, estimated closing costs, and how long you plan to stay, and I’ll calculate it directly.
