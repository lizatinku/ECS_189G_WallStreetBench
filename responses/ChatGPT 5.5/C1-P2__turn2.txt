--- PROMPT ---
What is the smallest amount of additional data that would meaningfully change your reasoning?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The smallest useful addition would be the **exact six-week date range plus one broad risk-appetite benchmark**, such as the **S&P 500/Nasdaq and VIX** over the same period.

That would meaningfully change the reasoning because:

* **If Bitcoin fell while equities also fell and VIX rose**, the “risk-off” interpretation becomes much stronger.
* **If Bitcoin fell while equities were stable/rising and VIX was calm**, then the Bitcoin move looks more likely **crypto-specific**, not evidence of a broad safe-haven shift.
* **If gold rose while real yields fell or geopolitical stress increased**, gold’s rise looks more like classic safe-haven behavior.
* **If gold rose for technical/central-bank/dollar reasons**, then the gold-Bitcoin divergence says less about investor fear.

So the absolute minimum I’d want is:

**1. The exact dates**
**2. S&P 500 or Nasdaq performance**
**3. VIX movement**

With only those, I could already distinguish between:

**“Investors broadly moved away from risk”**
versus
**“Bitcoin had a crypto-specific selloff while gold moved for unrelated reasons.”**
