--- PROMPT ---
What is the smallest amount of additional data that would meaningfully change your reasoning?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The smallest useful addition would be **the exact six-week dates**.

With just the dates, I could check what kind of market regime it was: broad risk-off, crypto-specific stress, rate shock, dollar move, geopolitical event, ETF-flow reversal, etc. Without dates, the same Bitcoin/gold divergence can mean several different things.

The next-smallest data point that would meaningfully change the reasoning is:

**How the Nasdaq/S&P 500 and real yields moved over the same six weeks.**

That gives a quick test:

If Bitcoin fell while stocks also fell and gold rose, the “Bitcoin traded like a risk asset; gold acted more defensive” interpretation gets stronger.

If Bitcoin fell while stocks were flat or rising, I’d look harder for crypto-specific causes.

If gold rose because real yields fell or the dollar weakened, the gold move may not be “safe haven” behavior so much as a rates/currency response.

If Bitcoin fell despite a favorable macro backdrop for risk assets, that weakens any broad investor-risk-off explanation.

So the minimum meaningful dataset is: **exact dates + one broad risk benchmark + real yields or the dollar**. Even that would not prove rotation from Bitcoin into gold, but it would sharply narrow the explanation.
