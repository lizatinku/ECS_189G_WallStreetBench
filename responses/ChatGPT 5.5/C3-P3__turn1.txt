=== Prompt ID: C3-P3  |  Category: C3  |  Turn: 1 ===

--- PROMPT ---
What would have to be true for the acquirer's stock to rise on the announcement instead?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
For the acquirer’s stock to rise, the market would need to believe the deal creates **net value for the buyer’s shareholders**, not just for the target’s shareholders.

That usually requires some combination of these things being true:

1. **The price looks attractive**

   The buyer is not overpaying, or it is buying assets the market thinks are worth more than the stated price. A low premium, distressed target, or strategically scarce asset can help.

2. **Synergies are large and credible**

   Investors believe cost savings, revenue gains, network benefits, or operational efficiencies are real, measurable, and achievable. In an airline deal, that could mean better route density, airport slot value, fleet utilization, loyalty-program benefits, or elimination of overlapping costs.

3. **Execution risk is manageable**

   The buyer has a strong track record integrating acquisitions, and the target’s fleet, labor contracts, systems, culture, and route network are compatible.

4. **Financing is favorable**

   The deal does not overly increase leverage, dilute shareholders, or strain liquidity. A stock rise is more likely if the buyer uses cheap financing, has excess cash, or structures the deal in a way that limits downside.

5. **The strategic logic is obvious**

   The acquisition fills a clear gap: geographic expansion, airport slots, regional feeder traffic, customer loyalty scale, or competitive positioning that the buyer could not easily build organically.

6. **Regulatory risk is low**

   Investors believe antitrust approval is likely and that required divestitures or remedies will not destroy the deal’s value.

7. **The market had already expected something worse**

   The stock can rise if investors feared a larger, more expensive, more dilutive, or riskier transaction, and the announced deal is better than expected.

In short, the acquirer’s stock rises when investors think:

**Value of target + credible synergies − purchase price − integration/financing/regulatory risk > 0**

for the acquirer’s shareholders.

That means the market is pricing the announcement as not merely a transfer of value to the target, but as a transaction where the buyer captures enough future upside to justify the cost.
