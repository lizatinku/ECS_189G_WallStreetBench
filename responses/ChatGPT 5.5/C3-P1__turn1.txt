--- PROMPT ---
Under what conditions would the same layoff news cause the stock to drop instead?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The same layoff news could cause the stock to drop when investors interpret it as a warning sign rather than a productivity move.

A stock is more likely to fall after layoffs when:

1. The cuts signal weak demand
   If layoffs suggest customers are spending less, subscriptions are slowing, crypto activity is down, cloud growth is weakening, or enterprise software budgets are tightening, investors may lower revenue expectations.

2. Management gives poor guidance at the same time
   Layoffs paired with reduced revenue forecasts, weaker margins, or cautious commentary often look defensive. The market may think, “Costs are being cut because growth is deteriorating.”

3. The company cuts growth-critical teams
   If layoffs affect engineering, AI, cloud, cybersecurity, sales, or customer support teams that drive future revenue, investors may worry the company is sacrificing long-term growth for short-term savings.

4. The layoffs appear large or repeated
   One round can look like discipline. Multiple rounds can look like instability, overhiring, or deeper structural problems.

5. The company still has high expenses despite the cuts
   If investors believe the layoffs are too small to fix profitability, the news may not improve confidence.

6. The company’s strategy looks confused
   For example, saying “we are investing aggressively in AI” while cutting key technical roles could raise doubts about execution.

7. Employee morale or retention risk becomes a concern
   Layoffs can cause top talent to leave, reduce productivity, or hurt the company’s ability to innovate.

8. The market expected stronger performance
   Even “cost-saving” layoffs can disappoint if investors were expecting growth, not restructuring.

So the basic difference is this:

Stock rises when layoffs look like efficiency.
Stock falls when layoffs look like distress.

For Coinbase, Oracle, Atlassian, or similar technology companies, investors would react negatively if layoffs made them believe the company’s core business is slowing faster than expected, or that AI investment is not enough to offset weakness elsewhere.
