--- PROMPT ---
Mortgage interest rates have increased by over 3 percentage points from early-2021 lows, peaking near 8% in late 2023 and sitting around 6.37% as of May 2026. How could this affect housing demand, monthly mortgage payments, and home affordability? Discuss any uncertainty or additional factors that may influence the outcome.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The increase in mortgage interest rates has had multifaceted effects on housing demand, monthly payments, 
and home affordability, alongside uncertainties that may influence the outcome. Here's a structured 
breakdown:

### **1. Housing Demand**
- **Reduced Demand**: Higher interest rates increase the cost of borrowing, reducing people's incentive to 
take out mortgages. This could slow down home construction, as buyers may prioritize other housing options 
or reduce their willingness to invest in new homes. However, delays in new housing availability could still 
drive demand for existing properties.
- **Uncertainty**: If interest rates stabilize or increase unpredictably, the overall affordability of 
housing might fluctuate, affecting demand for both new and existing homes.

### **2. Monthly Mortgage Payments**
- **Higher Payments**: With rising rates, both principal and interest payments increase. This could lead to 
a decrease in demand for home equity, as buyers may cut back on their mortgages or switch to alternative 
housing options like rental properties. However, if the rate rises too much, this could discourage home 
purchases.
- **Uncertainty**: If the rate increases too rapidly, it may lead to a temporary slowdown in home 
construction, but if it stabilizes, demand could remain stable.

### **3. Home Affordability**
- **Reduced Affordability**: Higher interest rates make home ownership more expensive, reducing the number 
of people who can afford homes. This could lead to a decrease in home affordability, but if new housing is 
available or prices fall, demand may still be there.
- **Uncertainty**: Factors like economic forecasts, policy interventions, or global economic conditions 
could influence whether affordability improves or worsens.

### **Additional Uncertainties**
- **Economic Conditions**: If the economy is recovering, there may be a temporary boost in demand. However, 
if there's a slowdown, affordability could worsen.
- **Policy Changes**: If governments or central banks implement policies to stabilize or reduce interest 
rates, this could mitigate the impact on housing affordability.
- **Global Economic Factors**: Inflation or geopolitical events could further affect the cost of housing, 
adding uncertainty to the situation.

In summary, while the overall housing market may experience a slowdown due to increased interest rates, 
uncertainties such as economic conditions and policy interventions could either stabilize or worsen home 
affordability.
