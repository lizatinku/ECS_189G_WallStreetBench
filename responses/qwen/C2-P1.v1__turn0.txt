--- PROMPT ---
U.S. 30-year mortgage rates went from roughly 3% in 2021 to a peak near 8% in late 2023 and now sit at about 6.37%. What does this mean for housing affordability and household demand for homes?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
TThe U.S. 30-year mortgage rates going from 3% in 2021 to a peak near 8% in late 2023 and now at 6.37% 
reflect a significant shift in the housing market. Here's a breakdown of the implications:

### **1. Housing Affordability**
- **Lower Rates**: When mortgage rates drop, it becomes easier for individuals and families to secure 
affordable housing. This leads to increased demand for homes as people are motivated to purchase or rent.
- **Market Stabilization**: The rate transition from 3% to 8% (a 50% increase) signals a stabilization 
phase. With lower rates, the supply of housing is likely to grow, further reducing costs for buyers.

### **2. Household Demand for Homes**
- **Increased Demand**: As people can afford homes, their demand for housing increases. This leads to more 
competition among buyers and sellers, potentially stabilizing the market.
- **Balance in the Market**: The combination of lower rates and growing supply may result in a balanced 
demand curve, where affordability and accessibility are key factors in driving demand.

### **Conclusion**
The rate decline from 3% to 6.37% has improved housing affordability, making homes more accessible to a 
broader segment of the population. At the same time, household demand for homes is likely to rise, driven 
by the availability of affordable housing and the lower mortgage costs. This shift signals a positive trend 
in the housing market, balancing affordability and demand for housing.
