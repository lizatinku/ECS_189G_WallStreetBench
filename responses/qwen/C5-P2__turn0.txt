--- PROMPT ---
Should I refinance my mortgage right now?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
Refinancing your mortgage right now depends on your current situation, financial goals, and the market conditions. Here's a structured approach to help you make an informed decision:

1. **Check Your Current Mortgage Rate**: Compare your current interest rate with the new rate you're looking to refinance. If the new rate is lower, refinancing could save you money in the long run.

2. **Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTVR)**: A high LTVR (e.g., 85%) may mean you're already paying more in principal, so refinancing might reduce your monthly payments if you can negotiate better terms.

3. **Credit Score**: A strong credit score may improve your chances of getting a better mortgage rate, but it's not guaranteed. Ensure your credit is in good shape before considering refinancing.

4. **Loan Terms**: Review the new mortgage terms, including interest rates, payment schedules, and closing costs. Compare these with your original terms to ensure you're getting a better deal.

5. **Financial Goals**: If you're saving money now, refinancing could be beneficial. However, if you're planning for the future, consider the long-term implications.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Compare different refinance options to find the best fit for your situation. Use resources like mortgage calculators or consult with professionals to make an informed decision.

By considering these factors, you can make a well-rounded decision about refinancing your mortgage.
