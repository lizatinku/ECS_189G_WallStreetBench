Between 2021 and early 2026, the cumulative inflation rate in the United States was approximately 21.86%, based on the Consumer Price Index. How could this affect consumer spending, savings behavior, and stock market performance? Identify groups most affected and least affected.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
Between 2021 and early 2026, a 21.86% inflation rate in the U.S. had significant implications on consumer behavior, savings, and financial markets. Here's a breakdown:

### **1. Consumer Spending**
- **Impact**: Inflation increases prices for goods and services, reducing the purchasing power of consumers. This leads to decreased spending on essentials like food, housing, and healthcare.  
- **Affected Groups**:  
  - **Households with fixed income**: Struggling with reduced wages and rising costs.  
  - **Individuals with limited savings**: Allocating savings to safer assets (cash, bonds) instead of spending on goods.  
- **Least Affected**:  
  - **Individuals with high savings or disposable income**: These individuals are less impacted by inflation as they save more.
