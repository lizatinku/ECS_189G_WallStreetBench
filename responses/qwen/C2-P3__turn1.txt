--- PROMPT ---
Which assumption in your analysis is most fragile, and what would you do to stress-test it?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The **most fragile assumption** in my analysis is **rent stability**, as it directly affects the total cost 
of living. This assumption is critical because it determines whether the student can reliably afford the 
rent or match the mortgage payments. To stress-test this assumption, we can simulate scenarios where rent 
either **increases** or **decreases**, and evaluate how this uncertainty impacts the total cost.

### **How to Stress-Test the Assumption**
1. **Scenario 1: Rent Stability Assumed**  
   - **Scenario**: Rent remains constant at $1,800/month.  
   - **Impact**: Total monthly cost is $21,600, which is significantly lower than the $528,000 cost of the 
condo.  
   - **Uncertainty**: If rent increases by 4% annually, the student would need to adjust their rent to 
match the increased payments, potentially tipping the decision from renting to buying.

2. **Scenario 2: Rent Decreases**  
   - **Scenario**: Rent decreases by 4% annually.  
   - **Impact**: Total monthly cost drops to $21,600 × (1 - 0.04/12) = $21,000.  
   - **Uncertainty**: If rent decreases, the student's total cost would be even lower, making renting more 
viable. However, this scenario highlights the fragility of maintaining a stable income stream.

3. **Other Uncertainties**  
   - **Mortgage Rate**: If the student expects the mortgage rate to increase, their monthly payment would 
be lower than the rent.  
   - **Time Horizon**: If the student is renting for a longer period (e.g., 20 years), they would need to 
adjust their monthly rent to match or exceed the mortgage payment, which adds another layer of uncertainty.

### **Conclusion**  
By considering scenarios where rent either **increases or decreases**, we can stress-test the assumption 
that rent remains stable. This highlights the importance of **rent stability** and other factors like 
mortgage rates and time horizon in making the decision.
