--- PROMPT ---
If you observed long-duration tech rallying but credit card APRs flat, what would that imply about the transmission channel?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
The transmission channel that lags the most among the three is **the price of long-duration tech stocks**. Here's why:

- **(a) Credit card APRs**: These are directly tied to the Fed's policy rate. A reduction in the Fed's rate directly impacts credit card APRs.  
- **(b) Auto loan rates**: These are also directly linked to the Fed's rate. A cut in the policy rate would reduce auto loan interest rates.  
- **(c) The price of long-duration tech stocks**: This is a **long-term pass-through**. While it is influenced by the Fed's rate, it is not a direct result of the policy change. Instead, it reflects broader market sentiment, which may not be as immediately affected as the credit card or auto loan rates.  

The price of tech stocks lags the most because it is a market-driven, long-term pass-through rather than a direct effect of the Fed's rate adjustments. This means that while the Fed's rate cut impacts the credit card and auto loan markets, the tech stock price is a broader, indirect reflection of the broader market environment.  

**Explanation**: The price of long-term tech stocks is a pass-through that reflects broader market conditions, making it the least directly affected by the Fed's policy rate changes.
