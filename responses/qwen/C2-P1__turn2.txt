--- PROMPT ---
What macro variable would have to change to most effectively offset the affordability hit you described?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
To most effectively offset the affordability hit caused by a 6.37% mortgage rate, the **home price level** 
would need to be **adjusted**. Here's the breakdown:

### **1. How to Offset the Affordability Hit**  
- **Home Prices**: If home prices remain constant, the impact of higher interest rates on monthly mortgage 
payments would be minimal. For example, a $450,000 home with a 6.37% rate would still be affordable if home 
prices stay around $450,000.  
- **Adjustment**: To offset the hit, **home prices must fall**. If prices are kept high, the affordability 
gap remains. Conversely, if home prices decrease, the cost of home ownership becomes more manageable, 
reducing the overall affordability issue.

### **2. Key Macro Variable**  
The **home price level** is the primary macro variable that needs to be adjusted. By **reducing home 
prices** or **maintaining them**, the affordability hit can be mitigated. This directly affects how much 
the household can afford to buy the home, thereby offsetting the overall impact of higher interest rates.
