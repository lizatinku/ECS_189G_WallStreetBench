--- PROMPT ---
A graduate student is deciding between (i) renting at $1,800/month in a city with 4% annual rent growth or (ii) buying a $320,000 condo at a 6.5% mortgage with 10% down. Walk through the key factors that would tip the decision one way or the other, and where uncertainty matters most.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
When comparing the decision between renting at $1,800/month with a city growing at 4% annually or buying a 
$320,000 condo with a 6.5% mortgage and 10% down payment, several key factors and uncertainties must be 
considered:

### **Key Factors That Would Tip the Decision One Way or the Other**

1. **Total Cost of Living**:  
   - Renting at $1,800/month would mean a total cost of $1,800 × 12 = $21,600 per year.  
   - Buying the condo costs $320,000 upfront plus $208,000 in monthly mortgage payments = $528,000 total 
cost.  
   - The total cost of living (renting) is significantly less than buying the condo.

2. **Time Horizon**:  
   - Renting for a fixed amount over a 12-month period would be cheaper than buying the condo, which 
requires ongoing monthly payments over the same period.

3. **Uncertainty in Rent Growth**:  
   - If the city's rent growth is 4% annually, the student would need to adjust their monthly rent 
payments. This introduces uncertainty about whether the rent will remain stable or increase, potentially 
affecting the total cost.

4. **Uncertainty in Mortgage Payments**:  
   - The 6.5% annual mortgage rate means that the monthly payment would be lower than the rent. If the 
student expects the mortgage to grow, they might need to adjust their monthly rent to match or exceed the 
mortgage payment.

### **Where Uncertainty Matters Most**

- **Rent Growth**: If the rent increases by 4% annually, the student's total monthly payments could grow 
significantly, making renting less viable.  
- **Mortgage Payment Uncertainty**: If the mortgage rate increases, the monthly payment would be lower than 
the rent, which could tip the decision from renting to buying.

In summary, the decision hinges on the total cost, time horizon, and the likelihood of rent or mortgage 
growth, with uncertainty directly affecting the viability of each option.
