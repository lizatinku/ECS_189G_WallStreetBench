--- PROMPT ---
What would be a leading indicator that the oil shock is starting to flow through to consumer prices?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
A **leading indicator** that signals an oil shock (sudden or prolonged increase in oil prices) is likely the **price of energy or 
fuel**, particularly adjusted for consumer prices. Here's how it works:

### **Reasoning:**
1. **Energy Sector's Price**:  
   Oil prices directly impact consumer prices through transportation, energy, and logistics sectors. If oil prices rise, energy 
prices (such as gasoline, diesel, or electricity) also increase, which can signal an oil shock. For example, the energy sector's 
stock price may reflect oil price volatility, as energy prices are a direct result of oil prices.

2. **Time Lag**:  
   The oil price move typically takes 1–2 weeks to fully impact industries like energy or logistics. This delay allows investors 
to adjust to the shock before seeing broader economic consequences.

### **Conclusion:**  
The leading indicator of an oil shock flowing through consumer prices is the **price of energy or fuel**, which shows the initial 
effect of oil price changes on energy-dependent industries and consumer prices. This indicator captures the direct relationship 
between oil and consumer price movements, signaling the oil shock's impact.
