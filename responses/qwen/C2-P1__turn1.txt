--- PROMPT ---
Walk through a concrete example: a household earning $90,000/year considering a $450,000 home. What changes between a 3% and a 6.37% mortgage?

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
### **Concrete Example: A Household Earning $90,000**

**Original Scenario**:  
- **Monthly Mortgage Payment**: $1,815 (at 3% over 30 years).  
- **Home Value**: $450,000.  
- **Monthly Payment**: $1,815.  

**Change When Mortgage Rate Increases to 6.37%**:  

1. **Monthly Mortgage Payment**:  
   - With a 6.37% rate, the monthly payment becomes:  
     $ M = 450,000 \times \frac{0.0637 \times (1 + 0.0637)}{(1 + 0.0637)^{30} - 1} \approx 30,375 $.  
   - This represents a **$21,560 increase** in monthly payments.  

2. **Impact on Home Affordability**:  
   - The **increase in monthly payments** makes the home more expensive, reducing the number of people who 
can afford it. However, **if new housing becomes available or prices fall**, demand could still persist.  

3. **Home Demand**:  
   - With higher interest rates, home construction may slow, but demand for existing homes (especially if 
they are in high-demand areas) may remain.  

### **Uncertainty Factors**:  
- **Economic Conditions**: If the economy stabilizes, demand could rebound.  
- **Policy Interventions**: If governments reduce interest rates or implement policies to stabilize housing 
affordability, the situation may improve.  
- **Global Economic Factors**: Inflation or geopolitical events could further affect the cost of housing, 
adding uncertainty to the outcome.  

### **Conclusion**:  
The increase in mortgage rates directly impacts home affordability and monthly payments, but the outcome 
depends on factors like economic conditions and policy interventions.
