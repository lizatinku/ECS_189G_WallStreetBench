--- PROMPT ---
The Federal Reserve has cut its policy rate by 75 basis points over the past nine months. How might this transmit to (a) credit card APRs, (b) auto loan rates, and (c) the price of long-duration tech stocks? Explain which links are direct and which are indirect.

--- MODEL RESPONSE ---
(a) **Credit card APRs**:  
- **Direct link**: The Federal Reserve's cut of 75 basis points lowers the credit card interest rate. 
Credit card companies adjust their APRs directly in response to the Fed's policy.  
- **Indirect link**: Lower interest rates may influence other financial markets, such as the broader 
economy, which could indirectly affect credit card APRs through market demand or supply dynamics.

(b) **Auto loan rates**:  
- **Direct link**: The Fed's policy rate cut lowers auto loan rates directly, as auto loans are based on 
the same interest rate framework.  
- **Indirect link**: Lower interest rates may lead to increased borrowing, influencing auto loan rates 
through market demand or supply dynamics.

(c) **Price of long-duration tech stocks**:  
- **Direct link**: The Fed's rate cut lowers the yield on tech stocks (which are bonds), directly affecting 
their prices.  
- **Indirect link**: Lower interest rates could increase demand for bonds, indirectly increasing the price 
of long-duration tech stocks.  

**Summary**:  
- All three areas (credit card APRs, auto loan rates, and tech stock prices) are directly affected by the 
Fed's policy rate cut. The price of tech stocks is primarily affected by the yield on bonds, which is a 
direct link.
